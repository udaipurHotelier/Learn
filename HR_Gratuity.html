What is Gratuity?

Gratuity is a monetary benefit given by an employer to an employee as a token of appreciation for their continuous service.

Gratuity Act with Date

The Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 governs the gratuity payments in India.

Eligibility Criteria

Employees must complete at least 5 years of continuous service with the company, except in cases of death or disablement.

Which Companies Have to Pay Gratuity?

Any company with 10 or more employees is required to pay gratuity under the Gratuity Act.

Why Gratuity is Given?

Gratuity is given to reward employees for their loyalty and long-term service.

When Gratuity is Given?

Gratuity is paid upon resignation, retirement, termination, or in case of death/disablement.

To Whom Gratuity is Given?

Gratuity is given to employees completing 5 years of service, or to nominees/legal heirs in case of death.

Process of Giving Gratuity

Employee submits an application; employer verifies details and processes payment within 30 days.

Terms and Conditions

Gratuity is calculated based on the last drawn Basic + DA salary. Misconduct or termination due to dishonesty may affect eligibility.

How to Apply for Gratuity?

Submit a formal application with necessary documents like resignation letter or service certificate to HR.

Formula of Gratuity

(Last Drawn Basic + DA Salary x Completed Years of Service x 15) / 26

Time Required to Process

Gratuity is usually processed within 30 days from the date of application submission.

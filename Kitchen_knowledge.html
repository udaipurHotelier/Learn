Kitchen

Sub departments of Food Production

Main Kitchen: This is the large kitchen area where food is prepared for different restaurants or F&B service outlets. The food here is prepared for Coffee Shop, Room Service, & Snacks for Bar. The main kitchen has further sub-sections such as
Indian,
Continental,
South Indian,
Tandoor.

Pantry – Most of the orders from the pantry section, such as tea/coffee, sandwiches, salads, raitas, etc. are serviced. The pantry operates 24*7*365 days.

Bakery & Confectionery- All bakery items like cakes, pastries, chocolates, Muffins are made in this section.

Butchery – It is a section where all the raw meat supplies such as chicken, mutton, fish, prawns, lobsters, etc are received and cleaned, properly wrapped for further storage.

Banquet Kitchen– Bulk cooking for function happens at this section

Chefs area – Executive chef sits here and a lot of planning takes place inside this area

Garde Manager: It is also known as salad house our Factory outlet for salad preparation in bulk. Here cold salads are prepared for Banquet parties and coffee shop buffet.

Commissary: It is a place or section where we store all the green vegetables in bulk.

Walkins : Depending upon the size of the hotel there is a minimum of 2/3 walk-in refrigerators. One is kept especially at -18 to store meat.


Some knowledge that is important in the kitchen of a hotel includes:

Food safety and hygiene: Adhering to strict food safety and hygiene standards is essential

Inventory management: Efficiently managing food and supplies is important.

Cost control: Carefully monitoring food costs is essential for profitability.

Menu planning: Collaborating with the F&B department to develop appealing and profitable menus

Staff training: Ongoing training and development to enhance skills and knowledge.

Culinary skills: The ability to prepare and cook food, along with all the associated tasks vital for operating efficiently in a kitchen environment. Some basic culinary skills include knife skills, sauteing, roasting, blanching, and emulsification.

Protective clothing: Wearing clean and sanitized protective clothing like a chef coat, hat, apron, and trousers while working in the kitchen

Hand washing: Washing hands in between jobs with lukewarm water and detergent and patting dry

Tasting food: Using disposable spoons for tasting food and never tasting food using fingers.

Food handler practices: Food handlers should never chew gum, eat sweets, or touch their mouth and nose while cooking.

Kitchen Hierarchy
Chef

Chef

The head of the kitchen, responsible for overall management and food preparation.

Responsibilities: Oversee kitchen staff, menu planning, and food quality.

Executive Chef

Executive Chef

The top-level chef who manages the entire kitchen hierarchy and ensures smooth operations.

Responsibilities: Supervising kitchen staff, budgeting, and inventory control.

Chef de Cuisine

Chef de Cuisine

The master chef responsible for overseeing all aspects of the kitchen.

Responsibilities: Managing sous chefs and ensuring food quality.

Sous Chef

Sous Chef

The second-in-command who assists the head chef in managing the kitchen.

Responsibilities: Supervising staff and ensuring daily operations run smoothly.

Demi Chef de Partie

Demi Chef de Partie

Chefs may move into the role of Demi Chef de Partie after working as a commis chef, but before moving into a chef de partie position.

Responsibilities:Prepares, cooks, and serves food, and ensures high-quality food preparation. They also assist the chef de partie, and cook in a specific section of the kitchen. .

Chef de Partie

Chef de Partie

A section chef responsible for a specific area of the kitchen.

Responsibilities: Preparing dishes in their area and maintaining standards.

Commis Chef

Commis Chef

An entry-level chef learning the basics of kitchen operations.

Responsibilities: Assisting chefs and gaining experience in food preparation.

Apprentices

Apprentices

Individuals in training to become professional chefs.

Responsibilities: Observing and assisting in basic kitchen tasks.

Kitchen Support Team (KST)

Kitchen Support Team (KST)

Support staff responsible for maintaining cleanliness and organization in the kitchen.

Responsibilities: Cleaning, organizing, and assisting chefs as needed.

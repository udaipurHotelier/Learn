Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH) Act

ACT 2013

Definition of POSH Act

The POSH Act (Prevention of Sexual Harassment Act, 2013) is a law enacted to protect women from sexual harassment at the workplace. It establishes a legal framework to prevent, prohibit, and redress sexual harassment issues, ensuring a safe work environment.
For Every Women who has Visited a Work place , That means a women can complain it she faces sexual harassment in her work place or a place of another person.
POSH ACT is also Applicable to every public or Private establishment that carries out any , commercial , Vocational , Educational , Entertainment , industry , Financial Activies in the India.

What does the POSH Act do?

The POSH Act mandates organizations to form an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC), educate employees on workplace conduct, and establish procedures for complaints and redressal. It covers all forms of employment, including permanent, temporary, and interns, ensuring a harassment-free workplace.

What are the circumstances that constitute sexual harassment?

Sexual harassment includes any unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct such as:

  • Physical contact and advances
  • Demand or request for sexual favors
  • Sexually colored remarks
  • Showing pornography
  • Any other unwelcome conduct of sexual nature
  • Creating a hostile work environment
  • Unwanted gestures, jokes, or comments of a sexual nature
  • Threats related to employment status based on compliance
  • Repeatedly asking for dates despite being declined
  • Cyber harassment including inappropriate messages or emails
Who is a Respondent?

A Respondent is the person against whom the sexual harassment complaint has been made. The respondent could be an employer, coworker, supervisor, vendor, or any individual associated with the workplace.

Way to File a Complaint

The complaint can be filed by the aggrieved woman in writing to the ICC or LCC within three months from the date of the incident.

Who is LC (Local Committee)?

If an organization has fewer than 10 employees, a complaint can be filed with the Local Complaints Committee (LCC), which is set up at the district level.

Enquiry Process

The ICC/LCC will conduct an internal investigation, interviewing the complainant, respondent, and witnesses while maintaining confidentiality.

  • Submit 6 Copies of Written complaint with supporting documents.
  • Name and address of the witnesses
  • LC / IC will send one copy of the complaint to the Respondent within 7 Working Days.
  • The Respondent will Submit his / her Response to the LC / IC with supporting documents and list of witnesses with in 10 Working Days
  • The LC/ IC will begin the Inquiry and finsh it within 90 Days.
  • Every hearing of the Inquiry will have minimum of 3 IC/ LC member including Presiding officer of IC / Chairperson of LC.
  • If Respondent or complainant not appear for three conceliation hearing with out valid reason the IC / LC can terminate the Inquiry and pass an order EX-PARTE (without hearing to both the sides completely)
  • Before terminating the complaint in such a manner , the IC / LC will give a notice of Fifteen Days (15 days) to the parties.
Where to File a Complaint?

Complaints can be filed with:

  • Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) in the workplace
  • Local Complaints Committee (LCC) at the district level
  • National Commission for Women (NCW) or labor courts
How Much Time is Required?

The ICC/LCC must complete the inquiry within 90 days, and the employer must take action within 60 days.

What Happens in Case of a Fake Complaint?

If a complaint is found to be false, the ICC/LCC may recommend disciplinary action against the complainant. However, the Act ensures that complaints made in good faith are not penalized.

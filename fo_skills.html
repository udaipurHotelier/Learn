Front office Operations

Front office staff in a hotel need to have knowledge of the hotel and its operations, as well as be able to communicate well with guests and anticipate their needs:

Front office staff should prioritize guest needs and anticipate their preferences. They should be able to provide information, take messages, and solve guest complaints.

Front office staff should be knowledgeable about the local area and be able to help guests find their way around. They can also provide information about transportation options, such as taxis, trains, and buses.

Front office staff should be able to communicate with other hotel departments, such as housekeeping, food and beverage, and security. They should also be able to share information with these departments, such as arrival and departure reports.

Front office staff should be able to explain the hotel's rules and expectations, such as quiet times and the hours of operation for services.

Front office staff should be able to handle guest requests for special amenities or room supplies. They can forward these requests to housekeeping for prompt action

Front office staff should be able to update guest profiles and files.

Front office staff should be able to receive payments.

Front office staff should be able to handle phone calls and emails.

Front office staff should be able to manage multiple tasks at once, such as answering phones, scheduling appointments, and handling check-ins.

Front office staff should have basic computer proficiency, including knowledge of office software.

Front Office stff, you must have the ability to identify problems and use problem-solving skills to provide solutions. This is an important skill as it ensures the continuation of normal business operations.

Front desk professionals work closely with company employees and other front desk workers, depending on their place of employment to carry out daily tasks. Therefore, they must enjoy working as part of a team and understand how teamwork contributes to successful business operations.

How to Deliver a Guest Welcome Message

Guest messages can be sent in a variety of ways. The best one for your hotel will largely depend on what best represents your brand and speaks to your guests. If your hotel is limited-service or sustainability-focused, you may prefer to send messages via email or text message.

Email:- You can send welcome messages to guests via email, either before the guest arrives or shortly after they check-in.

In-room:- You can leave welcome messages in the guest's room, such as a personalized note or a welcome package.

Contactless check-in:- If you use contactless check-in technology, you can include a guest welcome letter as a part of the flow. This may be a quick and easy way to automate the process.

Guest messaging:- You can send welcome messages to guests via text message (e.g., SMS, WhatsApp etc.), which is a convenient way to communicate important information quickly and easily.

Hotel app:- You can send guests a welcome message through your hotel app if guests are signed up prior to their stay. This way you can send out push notifications or utilize chatbots or in-app messaging.
